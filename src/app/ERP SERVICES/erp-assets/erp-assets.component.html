Assets add values to your business and also increase company’s equity where the liabilities decrease the values and equity of the company. The more Assets of your company overcome the liabilities, and that brings the financial growth to your business. If your company is with more liabilities than assets, then you may be on the cusp of going out of your business.


Assets

Assets are the resources for your business that gives you the future economic benefit. Assets add value to your business where you can increase your company’s equity. The assets are classified to current assets and non-current assets.

Current Assets: Current assets are the items that are completely consumed or sold. Or the assets are entirely converted into cash within the 12 months. Examples are current assets as follows:

  • Cash and Cash Equivalence: Cash and Cash Equivalence includes coins, funds, bills, cash in bank, checks, etc.
  • Receivables: Receivables means accounts, rent, interest, receivable from the customers. Or due received from the employers, etc.
  • Inventories: Assets held for sale
  • Prepaid Expenses: Prepaid Expenses means money paid in advance like prepaid insurance, advertisements, rent, etc.

Non-Current Assets: Non-current assets are the long-term investments of the company, and the values will not be accomplished within the 12 months. Non-current Assets examples are given below

  • Long-Term Investment: Investments are made with the long-term process, i.e., investments in properties, stocks, etc.
  • Land: The Land area used only for business purpose.
  • Building: Building includes office factory, store, warehouse buildings.
  • Equipment: Equipment offers furniture, machinery, and all the office, computer equipment, etc.

Liabilities

Liabilities are the financial obligations of any business. Liabilities are also classified into current and non-current liabilities.

Current Liabilities: Current liabilities are the obligations or debts which are done within 12 months. Current liabilities are paid with the current assets. Examples of current liabilities are listed below.

  • Trade & Other Payables: It includes rent payable, notes payable, accounts payable, interest payable, etc.
  • Current Provisions: Current provisions are estimated as short-term liabilities which are measured reliably.
  • Short-term borrowings: Credit arrangements or loans which are in short-term nature.
  • Current tax liabilities: Taxes which are payable.

Non-Current Liabilities: Non-current liabilities are the long-term obligations. And the liabilities are turned into the noncurrent when they are not paid within the time duration (12 months). Non-current liabilities include the following

  • Long-term bonds, notes, etc.
  • Deferred tax liabilities
  • All other long-term obligations.
